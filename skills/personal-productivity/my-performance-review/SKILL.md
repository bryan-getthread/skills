# My Performance Review

Self-review prep from your own ticket history: the period's numbers, an evidence-based read on what you're genuinely good at and where you're leaving value on the table, and a ready set of talking points for the review or 1:1.

## When to use

- "Help me prep my self-review for <period>"
- "Pull my stats for the quarter"
- "What should I bring up in my 1:1?"
- "Be honest — what are my weak spots based on my tickets?"

## Steps

1. Confirm the review period (default: the last full quarter) and pull the member's tickets for it (search_tickets): opened, closed, still open, reopened, escalated in/out. Disclose result caps — a review built on truncated data must say so.
2. Compute the period picture, presented neutrally:
   - Volume: closed count, mix by category/board, time logged
   - Responsiveness: how quickly clients got first responses, and the worst silent gaps
   - Quality signals: reopen rate, escalations returned, sentiment on threads
   - Standouts: the hardest ticket resolved, the longest grind, anything a client explicitly praised
3. Derive strengths — claims backed by at least 2–3 concrete ticket examples each ("consistently strong on <category>: #A, #B closed in one touch with positive client replies"). No horoscope compliments.
4. Derive growth areas the same way, honestly but fairly: patterns, not isolated bad days ("threads go quiet after escalation — #C, #D sat 4+ days awaiting your follow-up"). Distinguish personal patterns from desk-wide problems the member can't fix alone, and say which is which.
5. Build the 1:1 pack:
   - Three headline wins with evidence, phrased in first person, ready to say
   - Two growth areas phrased as development asks ("I want to get better at X — could I pair on the next Y?")
   - Questions to ask the manager (workload, growth path, blockers observed in the data)
   - The one number to know cold, and the context if it looks bad ("closures dipped in <month> — I carried the <major incident> that month")
6. Offer formats: talking-point list (default), a written self-review paragraph per section, or the 3-line elevator version.

## Guardrails

- This is the member's own review of the member's own data — never run it on a colleague (that's the lead's coaching tooling, with its own guardrails).
- Every strength and growth claim cites real ticket numbers; no unfalsifiable traits.
- Honest means honest: do not launder a real pattern into fluff, and do not manufacture a weakness for balance if the data doesn't show one — say the data looks clean and where it has blind spots (tickets don't capture mentoring, projects, or interrupts).
- Numbers without desk context can mislead; avoid ranking the member against teammates — this skill sees one person's slice, not a fair comparison.
- The output is preparation for a human conversation, not an official appraisal; label it as self-review prep.
