# Personal Tone Profile

The companion to Personal Context Scaffold, for voice: a standing skill that encodes how this member writes to clients — tone, structure, banned words, signature — applied automatically to every draft so "write this nicely" comes out sounding like them, not like a bot.

## When to use

- "Set up my tone profile" / "learn my writing style"
- "Stop using em-dashes in my emails" — the third time they've said it
- "Draft replies the way I write them" (optionally: "here are three of my emails, learn from these")
- ESL members who want consistent polished English in their own register

## Steps

1. Gather the voice by the easiest route:
   - Best: the member pastes 2–4 real emails they're proud of (sanitized of client specifics); extract the patterns and play them back for confirmation.
   - Otherwise: a short interview — formal or casual? contractions? how do you open and close? what words or habits do you hate?
2. Fill the profile template below, playing back each inferred rule as a checkable statement ("You open with the first name, no 'Dear' — right?"). Only confirmed rules go in.
3. Save as the member's persistent skill (via the in-app skill tools when available; otherwise output the finished skill text for them to save). From then on, apply it to every client-facing draft for this member — and say so once, so they know it's active.

### The profile template

```
# <Member>'s Tone Profile

## Voice
- Register: <e.g. warm-professional; contractions yes; brief over thorough>
- Sentence style: <short sentences; one idea per paragraph; no walls of text>
- Empathy: <acknowledge frustration first on complaints; never mirror anger>

## Structure
- Greeting: "Hi <first name>," — never "Dear", never "Hello team".
- First line: answer or status first; pleasantries after, if at all.
- Closing: "<closing line>" + signature below.

## Banned words & characters
- Never use: em-dashes (—), "utilize", "as per", "kindly", exclamation marks
  in the first sentence, emojis in client email.
- Replace: "reach out" → "contact"; "issue persists" → "problem is still happening".

## Formatting
- Plain text for anything syncing to the PSA. Bullets only for 3+ parallel items.

## Signature
<exact signature block, verbatim>
```

## Guardrails

- The profile shapes *how* drafts are written, never *what* is claimed — facts, commitments, and technical content always come from the ticket, not the style.
- Scoped to its owner: never apply one member's voice to another member's drafts.
- Banned-word lists are absolute in client-facing drafts — a banned character appearing "because the sentence needed it" is a bug.
- Do not store client names or content from the sample emails in the profile; extract style only.
- Team or house style (if the desk has one) wins on conflict for external comms; note the conflict to the member once instead of silently overriding their preference.
