# One-on-One Prep

**When to use:** A service manager is preparing for a monthly 1:1 or a 30-day check-in with an engineer.

**What you get:** A concise, candid brief on ticket load, response and resolution patterns, client sentiment, highlights, and areas to improve.

## Steps

1. Cover ticket load: worked, opened versus closed, and any aging or stalled tickets called out individually.
2. Cover response and resolution time, noting whether delays are tech-side or client/vendor holds.
3. Summarize client sentiment across tickets that have data, with the best and worst interactions named.
4. Give two or three highlights of standout work.
5. Give two or three specific, actionable areas to improve.

## Guardrails

- This is for the manager's use only. Keep it direct and candid.
- Name a specific client or ticket only when it genuinely needs attention; otherwise aggregate.

## Consolidates

One-on-one prep, 1:1 meeting prep, 30-day ticket assessment, weekly team analysis, and manager wins-roundup skills.
