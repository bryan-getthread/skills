# QBR & SBR Prep

**When to use:** An account manager is prepping for a client QBR or SBR and needs talking points, not a client-facing deck.

**What you get:** A short, section-headed internal brief: volume, top recurring issues, high-effort tickets, sentiment, and project/training opportunities.

## Steps

1. Open with total volume for the period, opened versus closed, and overall activity level.
2. List the top four or five recurring issues ranked by impact, each with one plain-language example.
3. Identify the top five long-running or high-effort tickets, one line each, no IDs.
4. Summarize sentiment trends and what drove any low scores.
5. Flag opportunities: projects, infrastructure updates, or training that would cut volume or improve experience.

## Guardrails

- This is internal prep. Do not hand it to the client directly.
- Prioritize signal over completeness and keep to a tight word budget.

## Consolidates

QBR prep, SBR prep, and new-client onboarding-readiness skills.
