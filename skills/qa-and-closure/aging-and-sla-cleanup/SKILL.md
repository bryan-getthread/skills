# Aging, SLA & Follow-Up

**When to use:** Run on a cadence to catch tickets going quiet, approaching an SLA breach, or stuck waiting on the customer.

**What you get:** A prioritized list of at-risk tickets with a recommended action for each: nudge, escalate, or close with sign-off.

## Steps

1. Find open tickets with no meaningful activity in a set window (for example 14 or 30 days) and tickets nearing an SLA target.
2. Separate genuinely stalled tickets from those correctly parked on a scheduled date or active change.
3. For waiting-on-client tickets, draft the next follow-up in the client-facing voice, escalating tone across attempts.
4. For abandoned tickets past your attempt threshold, draft a friendly closure note inviting reopen.
5. Present the list for sign-off. Never bulk-close or bulk-send without confirmation.

## Guardrails

- Skip tickets with a future scheduled service call in a proper status.
- Flag imminent SLA breaches for immediate attention rather than folding them into the routine list.

## Consolidates

Close stale tickets, stalled ticket auditor, aging ticket workflow, no-response closure, SLA breach risk, and follow-up-on-waiting skills.
