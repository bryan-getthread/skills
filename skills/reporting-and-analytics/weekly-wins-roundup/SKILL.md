# Weekly Wins Roundup

Surface the week's 3–5 genuinely standout resolutions for the team meeting — positive-only, direct, and specific enough that the praise means something.

## When to use

- "Pull the wins for Friday's team meeting."
- "Who did great work this week?"
- A recurring positive-recognition segment for standups or a team channel.

## Steps

1. Confirm the period (default: the last full week) and search closed tickets across human boards, splitting per board/signal; disclose caps. Exclude junk/NOC boards and auto-closed statuses.
2. Hunt for win signals: positive client sentiment or explicit thanks in the thread, fast turnarounds on hard problems, long-aged tickets finally resolved, tough escalations landed, a save on an unhappy client, or unglamorous backlog grinding.
3. Select the top 3–5. Favor variety — different people and different kinds of wins across weeks, not the same senior engineer five times.
4. Write each win in 2–3 sentences: who, what the situation was, what they specifically did well. Direct and genuine — "closed the 6-week vendor saga at <client> and the contact wrote back to say thanks" — no superlative padding, no performative cheerleading.
5. Output as a ready-to-read list titled with the week. Offer a one-line version per win for a chat post.

## Guardrails

- Positive-only: no backhanded "wins" that smuggle in criticism, no comparisons between people, no "most improved."
- Every win must trace to a real ticket and real thread content — never invent or embellish praise; if the week was genuinely quiet, present fewer wins and say so.
- Skip wins whose details are sensitive (security incidents, HR-adjacent, client disputes) or summarize them without the sensitive part.
- This is recognition, not measurement — do not attach counts, rankings, or "X closed the most tickets" framing.
- Rotate deliberately: if someone was featured heavily in recent weeks and the candidates are close, feature someone else.
