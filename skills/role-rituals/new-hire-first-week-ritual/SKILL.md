# New Hire First Week Ritual

The new tech's own runbook (not the manager's): five working days to wire up the assistant, form the daily habit, prove first knowledge, and turn shadowing into notes that survive. Run it day one and check it daily through the week.

## When to use

- "I'm new here — set me up" / "run my first-week checklist."
- Day one of a new technician, after accounts and access exist.
- A trainer pointing a mentee at their self-serve setup.

## Steps

Spread across the week; each day's item takes under 30 minutes.

1. **Day 1 — Context scaffold** — run `skills/personal-productivity/personal-context-scaffold`: teach the assistant who you are — role, boards you'll work, mentor's name, clients you'll start with. This makes every later skill answer in your context instead of generically.
2. **Day 1–2 — Tone profile** — run `skills/personal-productivity/personal-tone-profile`: set how your drafts should sound (and your ESL preferences if relevant) so client-reply drafting is yours from the first ticket.
3. **Every day — Digest habit** — run `skills/personal-productivity/daily-digest` each morning, even while your queue is tiny. The habit is the point; by week two this becomes `skills/role-rituals/tech-morning-ritual`.
4. **Day 3–4 — First quiz** — take a starter `skills/training-and-enablement/product-knowledge-quiz` (scope: the boards and clients from step 1). Wrong answers are the week's reading list, pulled from `search_knowledge_base` — a miss is a pointer, not a grade.
5. **Every day — Shadowing log** — after each shadowing session, log 3 lines with `skills/training-and-enablement/new-hire-onboarding-coach` as the sounding board: what the issue was, what the senior did that wasn't in the doc, one question to ask tomorrow. Unlogged shadowing evaporates by Friday.
6. **Day 5 — Close the week** — review the log and quiz gaps with the coach skill; output the first-week card: setup done (scaffold/tone), digest streak, quiz result + reading list, top shadowing insights, and one goal for week two. Share it with your onboarding guide — it feeds their `skills/training-and-enablement/onboarding-guide-checkin`.

## Time-short version

Steps 1 and 3 — the scaffold makes everything else work, and the digest habit is the ritual's lasting artifact. Quiz and log compress to end-of-week; note it honestly on the Friday card.

## Guardrails

- This ritual serves the new hire — it is their tool, not surveillance; the week card is shared by the new hire, not extracted from them.
- Never bluff competence: the quiz and log exist to expose gaps safely in week one, when gaps are free.
- No client-facing sends this week without a senior's review, regardless of how good the tone-profiled draft looks.
- Ask-a-human beats guess-and-hope: the ritual's outputs should generate questions for the mentor, and that's success, not failure.
- Skip-with-note beats fake completion — a 3-day digest streak reported honestly beats a fabricated 5.
