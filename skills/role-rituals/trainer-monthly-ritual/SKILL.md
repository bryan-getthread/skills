# Trainer Monthly Ritual

The trainer's monthly loop: every mentee gets a real check-in, knowledge gets measured not assumed, the team learns one thing together, and the skills matrix stays true. ~2 hours, monthly.

## When to use

- "Run the monthly training pass" / "how are the new hires doing" / "training month-end."
- Start of month, on the trainer's enablement slot.
- After a hiring wave, to get all mentees onto the cadence.

## Steps

1. **New-hire check-ins (40 min)** — for each active new hire, run `skills/training-and-enablement/onboarding-guide-checkin`: ticket-evidence of progress (types handled, escalation rate, closure quality trend), blockers, and this month's stretch goal. Evidence first, then conversation — the check-in reads their actual work, not just their self-report.
2. **Quiz cadence (30 min)** — run `skills/training-and-enablement/product-knowledge-quiz` for whoever is due: new hires on their ramp schedule, plus any tech whose recent tickets suggest a specific gap (target the quiz at that gap). Record results where the trainer tracks them.
3. **Lunch-and-learn pick (20 min)** — run `skills/msp-business-operations/lunch-and-learn-planner`: pick ONE topic from this month's evidence (recurring-issue data, quiz misses, a new tool rollout) and schedule it with a presenter. Evidence-picked topics get attendance; calendar-filler topics don't.
4. **Skills-matrix refresh (30 min)** — run `skills/msp-business-operations/skills-matrix-builder` in refresh mode: update ratings that this month's tickets, quizzes, and check-ins justify. Matrix changes cite their evidence; feed material changes to the dispatcher's routing review (`skills/role-rituals/dispatcher-weekly-ritual`).
5. Output the monthly training card: per-mentee one-line status + stretch goal, quiz results summary, the lunch-and-learn (topic, presenter, date), and matrix changes made.

## Time-short version

Step 1 only, restricted to mentees in their first 90 days — early-ramp drift is the costliest to catch late. Quizzes, lunch-and-learn, and matrix slip with a note.

## Guardrails

- Check-in evidence comes from tickets, not vibes; but ticket stats never become a verdict without the human conversation — the ritual preps the conversation, it doesn't replace it.
- Quiz results and mentee struggles are private to the trainer/manager lane; the shareable card carries statuses, not scores.
- Skills-matrix ratings only move on evidence (tickets handled, quiz passed, cert earned) — never lowered on a single bad week.
- One lunch-and-learn means one, scheduled with a presenter — an unscheduled topic list is a skip, and is reported as one.
- Skip-with-note beats fake completion.
