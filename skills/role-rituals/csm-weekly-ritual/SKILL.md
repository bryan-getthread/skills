# CSM Weekly Ritual

The CSM's weekly account rhythm: reach out before clients have to, catch sentiment sliding early, walk into every call prepared, and keep a light eye on expansion. ~90 minutes at the start of the account week.

## When to use

- "Run my CSM weekly" / "what do my accounts need this week" / "prep my account week."
- Monday morning across the CSM's book of business.
- After time away, to re-establish the account picture.

## Steps

1. **Proactive pass (30 min)** — run `skills/account-management/vcio-weekly-proactive` across the book: which accounts get a proactive touch this week and what the touch says (an insight, a heads-up, a fixed thing they didn't know broke).
2. **Sentiment-decline watch (15 min)** — run `skills/account-management/sentiment-decline-watch`: accounts whose ticket sentiment or engagement is trending down. Any account flagged here gets promoted to a call or an owner this week — a flag with no action is a flag wasted.
3. **Meeting prep (30 min)** — for each client call on this week's calendar, run `skills/account-management/meeting-prep-brief`. Fold in anything steps 1–2 surfaced about that client.
4. **Expansion skim (15 min)** — run `skills/account-management/expansion-opportunity-scan` in skim mode: any account whose usage/asks suggest a service conversation. Note candidates; deep-dive only the strongest one.
5. Output the weekly account card: this week's proactive touches (client + one-line message angle), sentiment flags and the action taken on each, calls prepped, and at most one expansion candidate worth pursuing.

## Time-short version

Steps 2 and 3 — declining sentiment and unprepped calls are the two same-week risks. Proactive touches and expansion skim slip with a note.

## Guardrails

- Proactive touches must contain something real (a fixed issue, a metric, a relevant heads-up) — a contentless check-in email costs goodwill.
- Sentiment flags require evidence (scores, ticket refs, dates); do not put an account on a risk list on a hunch.
- Client-facing messages are drafts until the CSM approves them — never auto-send outreach.
- Expansion candidates stay internal; nothing sales-flavored reaches a client from this ritual.
- Skip-with-note beats fake completion; disclose result caps on book-wide scans.
