# Exec Monthly Ritual

The owner's month-end in ~90 minutes: the service story, the operational story, the money story, and one sharp question that moves the business — instead of three reports read passively.

## When to use

- "Run my month-end" / "give me the month in review" / "exec monthly pass."
- First week of the month, before the monthly leadership meeting.
- Board or investor prep where the month's narrative is needed.

## Steps

1. **Service story (25 min)** — run `skills/reporting-and-analytics/ceo-service-desk-brief`: the month's desk performance at exec altitude — what clients felt, what broke, what improved.
2. **Ops story (25 min)** — run `skills/reporting-and-analytics/coo-ops-review`: capacity, efficiency, automation leverage, where the operation is straining.
3. **Money skim (20 min)** — run `skills/finance-and-billing/agreement-profitability` in skim mode: the best and worst agreements by effective rate, and any agreement that crossed a profitability threshold this month. Deep-dive only what moved.
4. **One strategic question (15 min)** — synthesize steps 1–3 into exactly ONE question for the leadership team, with the data that prompted it attached (e.g., "our bottom-five agreements all share <pattern> — do we reprice, re-scope, or exit?"). Put it on the leadership agenda with an owner.
5. Output the exec monthly brief: three one-paragraph stories (service, ops, money), the strategic question with its supporting data, and where each full report lives.

## Time-short version

Step 3 (money skim) and step 4 (the question) — the two things nobody downstream will do for the exec. The two briefs can be read async; note the deferral.

## Guardrails

- The strategic question must be earned by this month's data — recycled strategy talking points don't qualify.
- Profitability findings name agreements and patterns internally; treat rate data as confidential and keep it out of broadly-shared channels.
- Three stories, one paragraph each — if the synthesis doesn't compress, it isn't understood yet; go back to the underlying report.
- Skip-with-note beats fake completion; each underlying skill's result-cap disclosures carry through to the brief.
