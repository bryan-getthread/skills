# AM Annual Account Ritual

Run once per client per year (anniversary, fiscal-year start, or pre-renewal): the deep look that weekly and quarterly rhythms are too close to see — what the relationship delivered, what next year costs, who actually holds the relationship, and whether the paper still matches reality.

## When to use

- "Run the annual review for <client>" / "it's <client>'s anniversary — prep the yearly."
- 90–120 days before a major agreement's annual renewal.
- New AM inheriting an account, wanting the full-year picture built once.

## Steps

Run per client — this is a per-account ritual, not a portfolio sweep.

1. **Business-value summary (45 min)** — run `skills/account-management/business-value-summary` over the trailing 12 months: incidents prevented, projects delivered, response performance, measurable outcomes. This becomes the spine of the annual conversation.
2. **Budget-planning brief (45 min)** — run `skills/account-management/budget-planning-brief`: next year's anticipated spend — refresh cycles, expiring warranties/licenses, roadmap items from `skills/account-management/it-roadmap-builder` output, and known growth. Clients forgive prices; they don't forgive surprises.
3. **Stakeholder-map refresh (30 min)** — run `skills/account-management/stakeholder-map` in refresh mode: who joined, left, or changed influence this year; where the relationship is single-threaded. Single-threaded on one champion is the finding that must never be skipped.
4. **Agreement review prep (30 min)** — compare the current agreement against the year's reality using `skills/account-management/renewal-prep` and `skills/finance-and-billing/agreement-profitability` (this client only): scope drift, out-of-scope work absorbed, seat/service deltas, profitability trend. Output proposed agreement changes as internal recommendations.
5. Output the annual account pack: value summary (client-shareable draft), budget brief (client-shareable draft), refreshed stakeholder map (internal), and agreement recommendations (internal) — clearly labeled which is which.

## Time-short version

Steps 1 and 4 — the value story and the agreement reality-check are what the annual meeting cannot happen without. Budget and stakeholder work compress to their top-three items each, with a note.

## Guardrails

- Value claims cite real tickets, projects, and dates — an inflated value summary destroys the credibility the annual meeting depends on.
- Budget numbers are planning estimates and say so; firm quotes go through the quoting process (`skills/finance-and-billing/procurement-quote-request`), not this ritual.
- Internal materials (profitability, stakeholder assessments, negotiation posture) never ship in client-facing drafts — the two-pile labeling in step 5 is a hard rule.
- Nothing is sent to the client by this ritual; it produces the AM's meeting pack.
- Skip-with-note beats fake completion; if the trailing-12-month searches hit result caps, say so next to the affected numbers.
