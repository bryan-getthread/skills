# Recurring Issue Client Briefing

Draft the message that gets ahead of a chronic pattern: it names the repeating problem honestly, shows you've connected the dots across tickets, and lays out a concrete plan to fix the underlying cause rather than the symptom. It converts client frustration into confidence that someone is finally solving it for good.

## When to use

- "This is the fourth time <client> has had the same outage — brief them on the plan."
- "Draft a briefing on the recurring printer/VPN/mailbox issue."
- A pattern of repeat tickets on one root problem needs a proactive client message.

## Steps

1. Establish the pattern from the ticket history before writing: how many times, over what period, what the common thread is. Cite the pattern only as far as the records support it — if you searched and there may be a result cap, say "at least N" rather than presenting an exact count as certain.
2. Structure the briefing:
   - **The pattern** — name the recurring issue plainly and acknowledge the repeat impact.
   - **What we've learned** — the direction of the root cause based on the record (state as assessment, not certainty, if unconfirmed).
   - **The plan** — the specific steps to break the cycle, with owners and rough sequencing.
   - **What we need** — any client decision, access, or budget approval required (route budget/cost to the AM).
3. Be honest that recurring means the prior fixes treated symptoms; frame the new plan as addressing the root — without blaming anyone.
4. Apply defensive writing per the Email Baseline Standard.
5. Present the draft with view_openDraft for review. Do not send.

## Guardrails

- **Count honesty.** Don't overstate frequency; if the search may have hit a cap, say so. Never fabricate a number to dramatize.
- **Plan must be real.** Only commit to steps that are actually planned and owned; no aspirational fixes presented as scheduled work.
- **Cause as assessment.** If the root cause isn't confirmed, present the direction as the current assessment, not fact.
- **No cost commitments** — route anything billable to the account manager.
- Degradation: view_openDraft is in-app only. Over external MCP, output the draft in chat labeled "DRAFT — review before sending."
