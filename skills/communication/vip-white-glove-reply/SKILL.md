# VIP White-Glove Reply

Draft a reply for a VIP contact that raises the touch level without lowering the honesty bar: prompt acknowledgment, extra clarity, a proactive next step, and a named human they can reach. The polish is in the care and responsiveness, never in over-promising.

## When to use

- "This is from a VIP — draft a white-glove reply."
- Replying to a contact flagged VIP/executive on a client account.
- A high-sensitivity contact needs an elevated-touch response on a ticket.

## Steps

1. Confirm the contact's VIP status and any account-specific handling notes before writing (see triage-and-routing/vip-priority-handling for how VIPs are flagged and routed). Read the full ticket thread.
2. Draft the reply with elevated care:
   - **Prompt, personal acknowledgment** — greet by name, acknowledge the specific issue immediately.
   - **Clear current state** — exactly where things stand, in plain language.
   - **Proactive next step** — what you're doing next and by when, stated as a real commitment you can keep.
   - **Named contact** — give a direct point of contact for continuity.
3. Keep it warm and confident but not obsequious; brevity still signals competence.
4. Structure and tone per the Email Baseline Standard (communication/email-baseline-standard).
5. Present the draft with view_openDraft for review. Do not send.

## Guardrails

- **Content-honest despite the elevated tone.** White-glove means faster and clearer, not looser with the truth. Only promise timings and actions that are real and achievable; never invent a fix or an ETA to placate a VIP.
- **No preferential commitments you can't keep.** Don't promise a service level the agreement doesn't support just because the contact is important.
- Never expose internal notes, other clients' details, or the reason they're flagged VIP.
- Degradation: view_openDraft is in-app only. Over external MCP, output the draft in chat labeled "DRAFT — review before sending."
- Builds on communication/client-reply; cross-reference triage-and-routing/vip-priority-handling for detection and routing.
