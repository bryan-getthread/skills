# Warranty Expiry Notice Email

Draft the notice a client receives when a device or batch of devices is approaching warranty expiry: what's expiring, what it means for risk, and the options — framed to help them decide, not to pressure a purchase.

## When to use

- "The warranties on <client>'s laptops expire next quarter — give them a heads-up."
- "Draft a warranty expiry notice for this asset."
- An asset record or ticket shows a warranty end date approaching.

## Steps

1. Confirm the facts from the asset record/ticket before writing: which devices, the exact expiry date(s), age, and current condition if noted. Only include devices whose expiry is confirmed — flag anything uncertain as `NEEDS:`.
2. Open with the plain fact: which device(s) and when the warranty ends.
3. Explain the practical impact in one or two lines: after expiry, hardware failures mean out-of-pocket repair or replacement and potentially longer downtime.
4. Lay out the options neutrally:
   - **Renew / extend** the warranty (route pricing to the account manager).
   - **Replace** the hardware, especially if aging (route to AM/procurement).
   - **Run to fail** — accept the risk, plan a break-fix budget.
5. Give a clear next step: "Reply and we'll set up a call with your account manager to weigh the options."
6. Structure and tone per the Email Baseline Standard (communication/email-baseline-standard).
7. Present the draft with view_openDraft for review. Do not send.

## Guardrails

- **No pricing.** Never quote renewal, replacement, or repair costs — route to the account manager/procurement. This skill informs and routes; it does not sell.
- **Facts from the record only.** Don't assert a device's age, model, or condition unless it's recorded.
- Present options neutrally; no fear-based pressure or artificial deadlines.
- Degradation: view_openDraft is in-app only. Over external MCP, output the draft in chat labeled "DRAFT — review before sending."
