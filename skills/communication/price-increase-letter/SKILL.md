# Price Increase Letter

Drafts the message nobody enjoys sending: a price change stated plainly, anchored to the value delivered, with a real effective date — and no apology-spiral that invites the client to negotiate the letter instead of the agreement.

## When to use

- "Draft the price increase letter for <client>."
- "Our rates go up <date> — help me write the notice."
- An agreement renewal includes a rate change and the client needs formal written notice.

## Steps

1. Collect the facts from the requester before writing — none of these may be invented or inferred: the new price or percentage, the effective date, what agreement/services it covers, and the notice period the agreement requires. If any is missing, ask; do not draft around a blank.
2. Optionally pull supporting context: recent delivered work via search_tickets (resolved counts, projects completed, response performance) to ground the value paragraph in this client's actual service history — not generic claims.
3. Draft per the Email Baseline Standard skill (communication/email-baseline-standard), in this order:
   - **Value first:** one short paragraph on what the relationship has delivered — specific to this client where the record supports it.
   - **The change:** the new price/percentage and exactly what it applies to, stated once, plainly. No burying it below the fold.
   - **Effective date:** its own line. If the agreement requires a notice period, the date must respect it.
   - **Honest rationale:** one or two sentences maximum ("rising vendor and labor costs," "expanded coverage under your plan"). True, brief, done.
   - **Next step:** who to contact with questions — the account manager by name/role, not "the team."
4. Tone check: confident and respectful. Exactly one expression of appreciation; zero apologies. Cut any sentence that hedges the decision ("we hate to do this," "we hope you understand") — the change is decided, the letter informs.
5. Present as a draft via view_openDraft, labeled **"REQUIRES ACCOUNT MANAGER APPROVAL — do not send."** State in chat that AM sign-off on numbers, date, and framing is the gate before sending.

## Guardrails

- **AM approval gate is absolute:** never send, schedule, or hand off this letter for delivery — pricing communication is a human-approved action, every time.
- Never invent or estimate numbers, percentages, effective dates, or notice periods. If the requester's date violates the agreement's notice period (when known), flag it instead of drafting around it.
- No apology-spiral: apologetic framing invites renegotiation of a decided change. One thank-you, no sorries.
- Do not offer discounts, grandfathering, or exceptions unless the requester explicitly provides them.
- Do not editorialize about other clients' pricing or name any other client.
- Degradation: view_openDraft is in-app only — over external MCP, output the letter in chat labeled "PRICE INCREASE DRAFT — AM approval required."
