# Service Tier Change Notice

Drafts the message confirming a tier move: what the client gains or loses in concrete operational terms, when it takes effect, and how support works from that day forward — welcoming on the way up, respectful and door-open on the way down.

## When to use

- "<client> upgraded to the premium plan — draft the confirmation."
- "<client> is downgrading to essentials next month, write the notice."
- A tier change is agreed and the client needs written confirmation of what actually changes.

## Steps

1. Collect from the requester: the current tier, the new tier, the effective date, and the concrete operational differences (hours of coverage, response targets, included services, what's now billable vs included). Do not infer tier contents from tier names — plans differ per desk; ask for the actual deltas if not provided.
2. Draft per the Email Baseline Standard skill (communication/email-baseline-standard):
   - Confirm the change and the **effective date** up front, in one sentence.
   - **What changes operationally**, as a short list in the client's terms: "support hours become <hours>," "after-hours calls are now included / now billed at <rate>," "response target for urgent issues becomes <target>." Every line is something the client will actually notice.
   - **What stays the same** — one line, so the change doesn't read bigger than it is.
   - How to reach the desk from the effective date, if the contact path changes at all.
3. Frame by direction:
   - **Upgrade:** welcoming, benefit-led — but factual, not salesy. No superlatives.
   - **Downgrade:** graceful and entirely judgment-free. State the new scope plainly, note what falls outside it and how those requests are handled (billable, best-effort, or referred), and close with a door-open line about revisiting the plan anytime. Zero guilt, zero "we're sad to see," zero winback pitch.
4. If anything in flight straddles the effective date (open projects, scheduled work), state explicitly which tier's terms apply — ask the requester rather than assuming.
5. Present as an open draft via view_openDraft for human review and send.

## Guardrails

- Never invent tier contents, rates, response targets, or effective dates — every operational claim in the letter must come from the requester or the client record.
- The downgrade letter is not a retention letter: no guilt framing, no unsolicited counter-offers, no "are you sure." If the desk wants a save attempt, that is a separate human conversation before this notice exists.
- Billing changes get stated as facts with the effective date; if the requester hasn't confirmed billing treatment for the transition period, flag the gap instead of guessing.
- Do not compare the client's plan to other clients' plans or name any other client.
- Degradation: view_openDraft is in-app only — over external MCP, output the notice in chat labeled "TIER CHANGE NOTICE DRAFT."
