# Weekly Client Update

Drafts the update that makes a client feel managed: what moved this week across their open work, what's pending and why, and what happens next — sent on cadence, not on demand.

## When to use

- "Draft this week's update for <client> on the ongoing project/incident."
- "Summarize <client>'s open tickets into a weekly status email."
- A long-running engagement has a standing weekly-update commitment.

## Steps

1. Gather the week's record via search_tickets: the client's open tickets (or the agreed project/incident subset), what changed on each in the last 7 days, what's waiting and on whom, and anything newly opened or closed. If a search may have hit a result cap, say so to the tech and scope the update to what was actually retrieved.
2. Draft per the Email Baseline Standard skill (communication/email-baseline-standard), structured for a 60-second read:
   - One-line headline: overall state in a sentence ("good progress this week — two items closed, one waiting on your sign-off").
   - **Progress this week:** the items that moved, each in one plain-English line (translate per communication/technical-to-plain-english as needed).
   - **In progress / waiting:** what's open, its state, and — where the client owns the next move — a gentle, specific callout.
   - **Next week:** what the desk plans to advance, without over-committing.
   - When the next update comes ("you'll get the next one of these on <day>").
3. Aggregate honestly: group small items ("plus three routine requests, all resolved") rather than padding the email into a ticket dump. Ticket numbers in parentheses for reference, not as the structure.
4. If nothing meaningfully moved, say so plainly with why and what unblocks it — a truthful "quiet week" update beats a padded one, and beats silence.
5. Present via view_openDraft for the tech/CSM to review, adjust emphasis, and send.

## Guardrails

- Every claim traces to the ticket record this week — no rounding up progress, no "on track" for stalled work. The weekly update is where trust compounds or leaks.
- Client-owned blockers are stated factually and kindly, never as blame ("waiting on the network diagram from your team — no rush, but it's the next unblock").
- Never surface internal notes, tech names in a negative light, other clients, or internal SLA mechanics.
- Commitments in the "next week" section follow the confirmed-commitment rule: only what the responsible tech would actually sign up for.
- Result-cap honesty: never present a possibly-truncated ticket sweep as complete.
- Degradation: view_openDraft is in-app only — over external MCP, output the draft in chat labeled "DRAFT."
