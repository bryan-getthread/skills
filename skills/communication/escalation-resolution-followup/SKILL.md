# Escalation Resolution Follow-Up

Draft the message a client receives once an escalated issue is resolved: a clear account of what happened and what fixed it, a genuine acknowledgment of the impact, and the steps that reduce the chance of it recurring. It rebuilds trust after a rough experience.

## When to use

- "This escalation is finally resolved — draft the follow-up to the client."
- "Close the loop with <client> after the escalation."
- An escalated/complaint ticket has resolved and the client is owed a proper wrap-up.

## Steps

1. Read the full escalation history — what went wrong, how it was handled, the resolution — before writing. Every claim must trace to the record.
2. Structure the message:
   - **Acknowledge** — briefly and sincerely name the disruption/frustration. Own the experience without a legal admission of fault.
   - **What happened** — plain, factual account.
   - **What resolved it** — the fix.
   - **Prevention** — the specific steps taken or planned so it's less likely to recur (only real ones).
   - **Direct line** — offer a direct contact for anything that resurfaces.
3. Keep the tone warm, accountable, and measured — not grovelling, not defensive.
4. Apply defensive writing per the Email Baseline Standard: facts not speculation; no unconfirmed cause; no blaming a vendor/colleague/prior tech.
5. Present the draft with view_openDraft for review. Do not send.

## Guardrails

- **Acknowledge without admitting liability.** Own the client's experience ("we know this disruption was frustrating") without a written admission of fault or negligence.
- **Prevention must be real.** Only cite preventive steps actually taken or committed.
- **No compensation offers.** Do not offer credits, refunds, or discounts — route those to the account manager.
- Degradation: view_openDraft is in-app only. Over external MCP, output the draft in chat labeled "DRAFT — review before sending."
- See escalation/escalation-prep and communication/escalation-notice-client (the notice sent *during* an escalation). For a client-safe cause write-up use communication/client-rca-summary.
