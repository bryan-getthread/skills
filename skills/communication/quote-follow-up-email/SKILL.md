# Quote Follow-Up Email

Draft a light-touch follow-up when a quote or estimate hasn't had a response: a friendly check-in that offers help and makes it easy to move forward, without nagging. The quote is a sales-owned asset, so this skill never re-quotes or discounts.

## When to use

- "We sent <client> a quote last week and haven't heard back — nudge them."
- "Draft a follow-up on the outstanding estimate."
- A quote/opportunity is open and awaiting client response.

## Steps

1. Confirm from the record what quote is outstanding, when it was sent, and who owns it (sales/AM) before writing. Do not restate line items or prices from memory — reference the quote, don't reproduce or change it.
2. Draft a short check-in:
   - Reference the quote plainly (what it was for, when sent).
   - Offer to answer questions or walk through it.
   - Make the next step easy: reply, a quick call, or approval.
3. Keep it warm and no-pressure — one gentle nudge, not a countdown.
4. If the quote has an expiry or the pricing is time-bound, you may note it factually only if that's recorded on the quote — otherwise leave timing out.
5. Structure and tone per the Email Baseline Standard (communication/email-baseline-standard).
6. Present the draft with view_openDraft for review. Do not send.

## Guardrails

- **Sales-owner aware.** The deal belongs to the sales/account owner. This skill drafts a follow-up; it does not negotiate, discount, change terms, or re-issue the quote. If a sales owner is identifiable, the follow-up should come from or reference them.
- **No new pricing or terms.** Never introduce a discount, a new price, or altered scope to "close" it.
- No pressure tactics or fabricated scarcity.
- Degradation: view_openDraft is in-app only. Over external MCP, output the draft in chat labeled "DRAFT — review before sending."
- See sales-and-quoting/quote-preparation for building the quote and communication/followup-chaser for the general non-response chase pattern.
